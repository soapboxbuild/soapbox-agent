## Determining factors

- **Lease structure** — retail skews heavily **NNN**, especially for anchors and pad sites:
  - **NNN:** tenant pays its own utilities directly (usually separately metered) plus a
    pro-rata share of **CAM** (common area maintenance), which includes common-area utilities
    → landlord share of variable utility cost is **low**.
  - **Modified gross / gross:** less common in retail; utilities included with recovery via
    CAM or expense stop.
- **CAM recovery mechanics** — common-area utilities (parking-lot lighting, shared HVAC,
  landscaping/irrigation) are billed back through CAM on a pro-rata basis. Determine the CAM
  pool and each tenant's share; the **net** landlord share (after CAM recovery) is the
  savings basis. Watch for CAM caps and gross-up provisions.
- **Who pays which fuel** — in-store electric/gas almost always separately metered to the
  tenant (**tenant-paid**); common-area electric (site lighting), shared water/irrigation,
  and any central HVAC are owner-fronted and CAM-recovered.
- **Format** — strip/neighborhood center (mostly separately metered small shops), power
  center (large-box NNN), enclosed mall (large owner-operated common systems → higher gross
  landlord share, CAM-recovered). Format sets how much load sits in common areas.
- **Vacancy** — vacant units' utilities revert to the owner with no CAM offset.

Search the asset's leases/OM/rent roll and CAM reconciliations first, apply jurisdiction
rules, then finalize per fuel. Mark PRESUMED, low confidence, until lease structure and CAM
recovery are confirmed by the owner.
