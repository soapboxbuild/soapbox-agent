# Asset Type: Multifamily (shipped — full method)

The multifamily landlord/tenant split is driven by **how units are metered** and **which
systems are central**, bounded by **jurisdiction RUBS/pass-through rules**, and confirmed by
the asset's **documents** and **live listings**. Combine all of these — do not stop at
building form.

## (a) Building form → metering presumption

| Form | Typical metering & plant | Presumed landlord share |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Garden-style** (2–3 stories, surface parking, dispersed buildings) | Units almost always **individually metered** for electricity, often for gas; residents billed directly by the utility | **LOW** — most electric/gas cost sits with residents; owner typically pays only common-area electric, exterior lighting, and (if present) central water heating and irrigation/common water |
| **Mid-rise** (4–7 stories) | Mixed — in-unit electric usually individually metered; heating/hot water frequently **central** (owner-paid); water often master-metered | **MODERATE** — owner commonly pays central gas heat/DHW and master water; residents pay in-unit electric |
| **High-rise** (8+ stories) | **Central plants** (central boiler/chiller, house-metered common systems, central DHW); in-unit electric may still be submetered but a large share is house-metered | **HIGHER** — owner carries central heating/cooling/DHW and substantial house load; per-fuel split can still favor residents for in-unit electric |

Building form gives a **PRESUMED** split. It is a starting point, corrected by the evidence
below — never the final answer on its own.

## (b) Jurisdiction RUBS / pass-through rules

Even a master-metered building may bill utilities back to residents where a **Ratio Utility
Billing System (RUBS)** or submetering is permitted; where it is barred or capped, the cost
stays with the owner. Determine for this jurisdiction:

- Is submetering allowed, and is direct-metering by the utility the norm?
- Is **RUBS** permitted, and under what allocation formula (by occupancy, square footage,
  bedroom count)? Are there caps, administrative-fee limits, or tenant-consent requirements?
- Are any pass-throughs prohibited (e.g. some jurisdictions bar billing back common-area or
  certain water/sewer charges)?

A RUBS-billed building means the owner **fronts** the master bill but **recovers** most of it
— the *net* landlord share is low even though the meter is in the owner's name. Capture both
the gross (who the utility bills) and the net (who bears the cost after recovery); the
**savings basis is the net**. Search the reference library first, then web search; cite each
rule with its source and URL.

## (c) The asset's own documents (search FIRST, per core method)

Leases and lease abstracts, the OM, PCAs, energy/water audits, T-12 operating statements,
and utility bills routinely state responsibility. Multifamily-specific tells:

- Lease "Utilities" clause naming which fuels the resident pays.
- A **RUBS line item** or "utility reimbursement" income line on the T-12/OM → residents are
  billed back (net landlord share low for that fuel).
- Audit/PCA metering description ("individually metered", "master metered", "house meter").
- Utility bills addressed to the property (master) vs. absent from the file (individually
  billed to residents). A fuel stated in a document is **CONFIRMED**.

## (d) Live leasing evidence

Pull the property's current listings (apartments.com, the property's own site, Zillow
rentals). Multifamily listings almost always state **"utilities included"** or itemize
**"resident pays: electric / gas / water"**. This is current, market-facing confirmation of
what residents actually pay and frequently confirms or corrects the building-form
presumption. Cite the listing and its URL.

## Per-fuel resolution (multifamily norms)

Resolve each fuel independently:

- **Electricity** — in individually-metered garden/mid-rise, residents pay in-unit electric;
  owner pays common-area/exterior/house electric. Landlord share typically **low** unless
  house load is large (elevators, central cooling).
- **Gas** — if central heating/DHW, owner-paid (**higher** landlord share); if in-unit gas
  furnaces/water heaters individually metered, resident-paid (**low**). Determine whether gas
  serves central plant or in-unit equipment — this is often the largest swing.
- **Water/sewer** — very often **master-metered and owner-paid**, unless RUBS-billed back.
  Landlord share **high** absent RUBS; **low net** with RUBS recovery.

Then produce the output contract (per-fuel table, confidence note, owner question) and record
the verifier finding as the core method directs.
