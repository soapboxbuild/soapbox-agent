## Determining factors

- **Lease structure** — the dominant driver for office:
  - **NNN (triple-net):** tenant reimburses operating costs including utilities → landlord
    share of variable utility cost is **low**; owner still bears vacancy-period utilities.
  - **Modified gross:** utilities split per lease — often base-year or a defined set of
    services included, overages billed to tenant.
  - **Full-service gross (FSG):** rent includes utilities → owner pays utilities directly,
    recovering via an **expense stop / base-year** mechanism (tenant pays increases over the
    base year). Landlord share of the utility bill is **high** at the meter; net share
    depends on the expense-stop recovery.
- **Recovery / expense-stop mechanics** — for gross and modified-gross leases, determine the
  base year / expense stop and the pro-rata share billed back. The **net** landlord share
  (after recoveries) is the savings basis, not the gross meter bill.
- **Who pays which fuel** — separately metered tenant electric (common in office) vs.
  house-metered HVAC/central plant (owner). After-hours HVAC is frequently a separate
  tenant charge.
- **Metering** — direct tenant meters, submeters, or house meter with pro-rata allocation.
- **Multi-tenant vs single-tenant** — single-tenant NNN often pushes nearly all utility cost
  to the tenant; multi-tenant leaves common systems and vacant space with the owner.
