## Determining factors

- **Lease structure** — industrial is predominantly **NNN**, often long-term single-tenant:
  - **NNN (single-tenant):** tenant pays essentially all utilities directly (separately
    metered) → landlord share of variable utility cost is **very low**; owner bears little
    beyond structure.
  - **Multi-tenant NNN:** each tenant separately metered where possible; shared/common
    utilities billed back pro-rata.
  - **Gross / modified gross:** rare in industrial; utilities included with expense-stop
    recovery.
- **Process vs. building load** — a large share of industrial utility use is **tenant
  process load** (manufacturing equipment, cold storage, compressed air), which is
  separately metered and tenant-paid. Distinguish process load from base-building load;
  retrofit savings on process equipment accrue to the tenant, not the owner.
- **Who pays which fuel** — tenant electric/gas for process and space conditioning is
  usually direct-metered (**tenant-paid**); owner typically retains only common-area/site
  lighting, fire/life-safety, and shared water. Landlord share is generally the **lowest** of
  all asset types.
- **Metering** — direct utility meters per tenant/bay are the norm; house meters cover only
  minimal common systems.
- **Building type** — warehouse/distribution (low base-building load, high tenant-metered
  lighting/HVAC), manufacturing (dominant tenant process load), flex/R&D (more common
  systems, slightly higher owner share).
